The Benefits of TARI®

• Increased Profit

TARI (Target Average Rate Index) is a benchmark focused on the "key driver" (key activity of the business). TARI provides the focus and the means of tracking the bottom-line impact of all business transactions.

Thus TARI painlessly and dynamically improves the bottom line profit.

• Cash Saved

TARI stops the seepage of cash resulting from unprofitable transactions.

• Competitive Advantage

TARI provides a clear advantage over competitors. Being immediately aware of the impact on the targeted bottom line of every quote or invoice or transaction, gives the business a competitive edge.

• Affordable

TARI costs just US$27.75 per month by subscription.

• Applicable to All

TARI applies to all business types (service, retail, wholesale, manufacturing, trades, professional) and all sizes from small to medium to large. If the business sells something - anything - then TARI can help.

• Improved Productivity

Despite everyone being busy and stressed, a typical business is only 64% or so productive i.e. 5 hours output for 8 hours pay. Because it is not possible to improve on the unknown, by identifying actual productivity, TARI starts the process of increasing it. Getting the productivity up to 75%, or just 6 hours output in an 8 hour day, can make a dramatic difference, the difference between dying and thriving.

• Increased Output

Improved productivity achieved as a result of TARI means increased output with the same resources. The value of the extra output, less any materials cost, is pure additional profit.

• Reduced Stress

By immediately identifying the impact on the bottom line of various options, TARI takes the stress out of decision-making, making it obvious which option out of several will be better.

• Better Pricing

TARI takes the guesswork out of pricing quotes, products, or services.

• Better Planning

TARI gives clear direction about the optimal pricing and volume of products and/or services.

• Immediacy

TARI provides immediate feedback of results compared with target.

• Clarity

TARI provides clarity by allowing a woods-eye view of overall performance at all times. (The details that so often get in the way remain in the background.)

• Simplicity

TARI is easily understood and applied by employees.

The Pulse of the business can be identified at a glance.

Business owners, managers, and employees, including people without accounting or management qualifications, can apply TARI themselves, and see their business transformed. However, for those who would rather contract out the task, or who feel they do need help, advisors at various levels can be found via TARI Network.

• Quick Results

You can start benefiting from TARI as quickly as 20 minutes from now. You can prove this yourself by taking a test drive of TARI using the free TARI Business Simulator. (TARI for Business does everything that the Business Simulator does plus automate the follow-up that makes the difference day by day.)

Any data you enter into the Simulator can be transferred to TARI for Business following subscription.

• Motivation

TARI infuses new life into people by encouraging a sense of purpose and an understanding of how each individual's contribution impacts the business.

• Continuous    Improvement Culture

TARI encourages a culture of continuous improvement as management and staff become increasingly aware of ways and means of improving business performance in real time.

All that is required to get started with TARI for Business is an estimate of expenses for the period ahead together with a target net profit. That 20 minute input sets your "GPS" permitting the TARI for Business software to guide you to the desired bottom line, nudging you back on track, day by day. The result is the step by step transformation of your business.

Only 3 figures are required each day or week to drive a very powerful feedback report known as the Key Indicator Report.

Department Setup

Setup, Department or Revenue Group (Business Structure)

TARI for Business uses "Departments" or "Revenue Groups" to handle different key activities within a business. This is where the "Departments" or "Revenue Groups" are defined, and edited.

Feature

Benefit

Departments/Revenue Groups A business can be divided into sections (Departments/Revenue Groups) so that the appropriate activity or key driver is being tracked for each part of the business. (Each department has its own key activity or driver).

Common activities are "Number of Hours Billed" or just "Hours", "Number of Sales" or just "Sales", "Ton Miles/Tonne Kilometers", "Gallons/Liters" and so on. It is possible to change and/or define industry specific activities if required.

For a gas station with a workshop and a convenience store, fuel sales would use "Gallons/Liters", the workshop would use "Hours", and the convenience store would use "Sales".

Each department can also have its own periods. Targeting and tracking is done by Department.
Separate or Consolidated Reporting Reports can consolidate (combine) departments, or show them separately.

Targeting Including TARI

Targeting is where Past Information - if available - is entered, and the actual targeting that sets the goals for the business is performed.

The outcome will provide the TARI, or Target Average Rate Index, for the business or a department of the business.

In the case of a retail/wholesale business, where number of sales is the key driving activity, TARI will refer to the Target Average Gross Per Sale (as measured by a cash ring-up or an invoice).

In the case of a manufacturing or service business, where hours billed is the measure of the key driving activity, TARI will refer to the Target Average Gross Profit Per Hour (where gross profit equals sales cost of goods or materials used).

Other targets such as for Gross Profit and Net Profit are also set here.

Feature

Benefit

Past Information TARI for Business allows easy entry of past Financial Data where available, ranging from one year to five or more to reveal trends.
What If The "What If" part of Targeting provides three scenarios for developing TARI and other targets. Select a base data source (past period or a previous target) for your "what if" and then set up 3 possible outcomes based on the most likely, pessimistic, or optimistic views of the period ahead. The results become a set of targets against which progress is measured.
Dynamic Targeting Targeting is dynamic and not confined to a financial period. This allows new targets to be set at any time to reflect changes in the business due to a changed economic environment, or new competitive threats.
No Fixed Period TARI for Business can be set up at any time and the dates specifying when a period starts and ends (up to a year in a period) changed as needed. When dates are changed, any data entered remains attached to its date and is available when next that date is included in the current period.
Flexible, Simple Chart of Accounts TARI for Business needs few accounts as it deals with generating income rather than tracking every expense transaction. That's for your transaction accounting software. TARI for Business focuses on generating profit and controlling productivity.

For the few accounts which are needed, a standard chart of accounts is used which you can add to, or modify to match the accounts used by your existing accounting software.
No account numbers to enter Entering information other than invoice data is achieved simply by putting an amount in a field, or using the mouse to drag a value up or down.

Tracking

Tracking is where business activity as it happens is tracked to compare progress with target. For TARI to work its wonders, sales data should be entered frequently, preferably daily, but not less than weekly. Monthly is OK for expense data.

Feature

Benefit

Fast Tracking Results Only 3 figures are required each day or week to drive a very powerful Key Indicator Report.
No Fixed Period As with Targeting, Tracking does not involve fixed time periods. Dates can be specified to suit when when a period starts and ends (up to a year in a period). When dates are changed, any data entered remains attached to its date and is available when next that date is included in the current period.
Improve Billing of Hours For any business selling labor hours, tracking spells out actual versus targeted productivity, and so prompts for meeting the targeted daily or weekly performance goals, either by improving the level of activity or the contribution per unit of activity.
Improve Goods Sales For any business selling goods, off or on the web, tracking spells out actual versus targeted daily and/or weekly sales volume and prompts for action to meet or surpass the weekly profit target either by improving the number of sales, or the contribution per sale.
Invoice Grouping by Quote, Personnel or Product Tracking performance of a job against a quote or products against a monthly sales target, provides a definitive advantage because unlike many sales budgets, it's based on a desired profit contribution not merely sales volume.

Technical Stuff

Finally, here are some of the technical specs about TARI for Business:

  • TARI for Business is a 21MB 32 bit Windows program written using Delphi and the FirebirdSQL Open Source Database. It runs on the XP, Vista, and Windows 7 versions of Windows.
  • Each business has its own licensed file - just one file to hold everything. This makes backup easy, as well as transfer to others authorized to access the data.
  • Access to the data is password protected, using one way encryption to keep the password secure.
  • A backup system is built in. You can backup your database from within the software, and even send it to someone else via email.
