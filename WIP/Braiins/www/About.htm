The Team

David Hartley

Following several years as a civil engineer involving extensive computing and mathematical modelling, David founded Hartley Computer (HC) in Brisbane Australia in 1974 and developed the Hartley Professional Accountants' System - HAPAS - which became one of the first mini computer vertical market successes in the world with 250 staff and 3,000 sites in 7 countries.

David sold his HC interests in 1985 and after a period in Hong Kong developing, with his partners, the world's first voice activated automatic fax switch, the BIT PHAXswitch, and winning two Hong Kong Electronics design awards in the process of growing the business to HK$40 million with exports to 35 countries, moved to the UK in 1993 to undertake a 21st century Windows equivalent of HAPAS.

By 1998, David and partners in Hartley Computer UK Ltd, had gained 1,000 Accounting Practice Clients ranging from one man practices to PricewaterhouseCoopers, mainly using the Accounts Production system named Hartley.Accountant.

Then, in 1999, David’s new wife Caramia was diagnosed with Primary Progressive Multiple Sclerosis. David and partners sold the UK business to Sage PLC, so Caramia and David could move to a warmer climate in Antigua, which Caramia thought would help her, as it has done.

Hartley.Accountant, renamed Sage Accounts Production Advanced (SAPA) remains the flagship product of the Sage Accounting Division to this day, some 11 years later, attesting to its fundamental quality.

In Antigua, David pursued various consulting and online business interests.

In January 2011 when the news broke that Sage has failed to release iXBRL capable version of its Accounts Production systems in time to meet the HRMC deadline of 1st April 2011, David and partners offered their services to Sage to help their old clients, and Sage, through the crisis. Plus it was an opportunity to earn a few bob. But Sage did not take advantage of the offer, and so Braiins was born, as a way to offer iXBRL Accounts Production services to SAPA users, without infringing on any Sage or SAPA proprietary information.
